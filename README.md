# Future_interns_DS_01
# Business Sales Dashboard from E-Commerce Data
# Overview  
This project presents a comprehensive Business Sales Dashboard built from E-Commerce data. The dashboard visually represents key business metrics such as sales, profit, revenue, quantity sold, and product performance across different categories and regions. It helps stakeholders quickly understand sales distribution, profitability, and product popularity to support data-driven decision making.

# Features  
- **Sales Breakdown by Category:** Visual pie chart showing sales distribution across Technology, Furniture, and Office Supplies.  
- **Sales by Segment:** Bar chart representing sales by consumer segments (Consumer, Corporate, Home Office).  
- **Sales by Product Name:** Horizontal bar chart highlighting top selling products.  
- **Quantity Sold by Category:** Donut chart showing quantities sold by category.  
- **Profit and Revenue Analysis:** Line charts demonstrating profit and revenue trends by category.  
- **Geographical Sales by State:** Map visualization depicting sales and profit distributions across different states.  
- **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**  
  - Total Profit  
  - Total Revenue  
  - Total Orders  
  - Number of Returns  
- **Filter Controls:** Region and Category selectors for dynamic data filtering.

# Data Source  
The dashboard is based on real e-commerce sales data, which includes transactional details related to sales volume, product categories, customer segments, and geographical locations.

# Technologies Used  
- Microsoft Excel for initial data collection and preparation  
- Power BI for creating interactive dashboards and visualizations  
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) in Power BI for calculating key metrics such as revenue, returns, and orders

# How to Use  
1. Clone the repository.  
2. Open the dataset in Microsoft Excel for any initial review or preprocessing.  
3. Load the data into Power BI Desktop to explore the interactive dashboard.  
4. Use the built-in filters to browse sales data by region or category.  
5. Leverage the DAX measures embedded in Power BI for revenue, returns, and orders calculations.  
6. Interact with charts and maps to gain insights on sales, profit, and product performance.  
7. Review the KPIs for a quick summary of business health.

#Insights  
- The Technology category contributes the largest share of total sales.  
- Corporate segment shows significantly higher sales compared to Consumer and Home Office segments.  
- The dashboard reveals best-selling products and areas with highest profits.  
- Quantity sold, profit, and revenue trends indicate cyclical business performance patterns.
